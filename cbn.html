Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN)

The Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) is the central bank and apex monetary authority of Nigeria. Established in 1958 and commenced operations on July 1, 1959, the CBN is responsible for the regulation of the banking and monetary system in Nigeria.

History

The CBN was established by the CBN Act of 1958, which was repealed and replaced by the CBN Act of 1991, and later amended in 1993, 1997, 1998, 1999, and 2007. The 1958 Act established the CBN as a corporation, with a capital structure similar to that of a private corporation, but with a public mandate to promote economic growth and development.

Mission and Vision

The mission of the CBN is "to promote a sound financial system in support of the economic development of Nigeria". The bank's vision is "to be one of the most efficient and effective central banks in the world, delivering price stability and promoting sustainable economic growth and development".

Functions

The CBN has the following functions:

  • Monetary Policy: The CBN is responsible for formulating and implementing monetary policy in Nigeria, with the goal of maintaining price stability and promoting economic growth.
  • Banking Supervision: The CBN supervises and regulates banks and other financial institutions operating in Nigeria, to ensure their stability and soundness.
  • Currency Management: The CBN is responsible for the management of Nigeria's currency, including the minting and distribution of coins and notes.
  • Financial Inclusion: The CBN promotes financial inclusion by implementing policies and programs to increase access to financial services for all Nigerians.
  • International Operations: The CBN represents Nigeria in international financial institutions, such as the International Monetary Fund (IMF) and the World Bank.

Governance

[8:31 PM]

The CBN is governed by a board of directors, which consists of the governor, four deputy governors, and nine non-executive directors. The governor is the chief executive officer of the bank and is responsible for the day-to-day management of the bank.
